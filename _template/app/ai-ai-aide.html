Sentence Patterns

Pattern Explanation Example from Song
Joga [description] que eu quero [infinitive] Imperative + purpose clause: "Play [X] because I want to [Y]" Joga bonito que eu quero ver
Joga pra [pronoun] que eu jogo pra [pronoun] Reciprocal action: "Do for me and I'll do for you" Joga pra mim que eu jogo pra você
Era [pronoun], era [pronoun] Identification in past: "It was [X], it was [Y]" Ô era eu, era você

🌍 Cultural Context

Aidê is said to be the name of an enslaved African woman brought to Brazil, renowned for her beauty and spiritual presence. Her story is embedded in capoeira oral tradition as a symbol of African identity and suffering under slavery.

The Song: "Ai, Ai, Aidê" is a corrido — a short, fast call-and-response song used to keep energy high during the roda (capoeira circle). It's among the most widely taught corridos in both Capoeira Angola and Capoeira Regional traditions.

Famous Definition: The line "Capoeira é malícia, é mandinga, é prazer" is one of the most famous definitions of capoeira, encapsulating its three pillars — cunning intelligence (malícia), spiritual/magical power (mandinga), and joy (prazer).

Notable Recording: A prestigious recording exists by Grupo de Capoeira Angola Pelourinho, produced by Mestre Cobra Mansa, released in 1996 on Smithsonian Folkways — one of the world's most prestigious archival labels.
