## Grammar Overview
- Main structures noticed: preterite (past) tense, imperative commands
- Verb forms noticed: subiu, desceu, pulou — 3rd person singular preterite; sobe/desce — imperatives
- Repeated patterns: "A maré [verb]" repeats throughout
- Useful connectors: pra (para = to/for), é de (is of)
- Unusual or poetic expressions: ioió / iaiá — traditional Afro-Brazilian call sounds
- Questions to review later: Why does the imperative use present tense form in Portuguese?

## Cultural Notes
- Ilha de Maré is a small fishing island in the Bay of All Saints (Baía de Todos os Santos) near Salvador, Bahia.
- Mestre Waldemar da Paixão, one of capoeira's most celebrated mestres, was born on Ilha de Maré — this song honors that heritage.
- Ioió and Iaiá are Afro-Brazilian affectionate terms with roots in enslaved communities in Bahia; they appear in many traditional capoeira songs.
- The tide imagery connects to Candomblé and the sea deity Iemanjá, deeply woven into Bahian culture.

## Repeated Expressions / Chorus
- "A maré tá cheia, ioió / iaiá" — main call line
- "Sobe maré" — chorus response to rising tide
- "Desce maré" — chorus response to falling tide
- "Vou pra Ilha de Maré" — chorus destination line
