Tips:
• Vou sounds like "voh," not "vow."
• The j in cajueiro sounds like the "s" in measure — a soft zh sound.
• Loiá is often sung with a long, open final vowel — let it ring.
• Cajuê and cajueiro are closely related — same root, different ending.

📖 Grammar

1. Near future with ir + infinitive

Form in song Base verb Tense / mood Meaning Notes
vou mandar ir + mandar Near future / intention I'm going to send / sing Vou is first-person singular present of ir.
vou mandar lecô ir + mandar Near future I'm going to call out lecô The whole phrase is one grammatical unit.
vou mandar loiá ir + mandar Near future I'm going to sing loiá Loiá is the object of mandar here.

Vou (no eu) Subject pronoun eu is dropped Vou mandar (not eu vou mandar) Portuguese often omits subject pronouns because verb endings carry the meaning.

🌍 Cultural Context

This is a traditional capoeira corrido, so it belongs to the call-and-response side of capoeira singing rather than a solo narrative song.

The words cajueiro and caju connect the song to the cashew tree, a familiar image in Brazilian life, food culture, and song tradition.

Different communities sing the song with small variations — including the spelling leco vs lecô and loiá vs loiô — which is normal and expected in oral tradition.

In capoeira, songs like this help organize the roda, support rhythm, and bring the group into one shared voice. The simplicity of the lyrics makes them easy for everyone to join.

The refrain words lecô and loiá are important because they keep the chant moving even when the literal meaning is flexible or debated. Their role is musical, not just linguistic.

This song is especially good for beginners because it has very few unique words and a highly predictable structure — once you learn the first two lines, you know most of the song.

🔁 Repeated Expressions

  • "Vou mandar lecô cajueiro" — Main call line; sets the chant and gives the song its name. Sung by the lead singer.
  • "Vou mandar loiá" — Main response line; completes the call-and-response pair. Sung by the group.
  • "Vou mandar lecô cajuê" — Variant call line; keeps the rhythm while shifting to the shorter refrain word.
  • "Vou mandar loiá cajuê" — Matching response variant; reinforces the musical loop with added energy.
