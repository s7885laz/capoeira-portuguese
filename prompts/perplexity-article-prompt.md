## Difficulty Assessment Methodology

### Vocabulary Complexity
- **Level 1:** 50-100 unique words, 80%+ high-frequency
- **Level 2:** 150-250 unique words, 70% high-frequency
- **Level 3:** 300-500 unique words, 60% high-frequency
- **Level 4:** 500-800 unique words, mixed frequency
- **Level 5:** 800+ unique words, specialized/literary

### Grammar Complexity
- **Level 1:** Present tense, simple imperative, basic adjectives
- **Level 2:** Present + preterite, comparatives, simple subordinate clauses
- **Level 3:** Multiple tenses, compound sentences, some subjunctive
- **Level 4:** Advanced subjunctive, conditional, literary structures
- **Level 5:** Complex conditionals, rare forms, dialectal variation

### Sentence Structure
- **Level 1:** Average 5-8 words per sentence, simple SVO order
- **Level 2:** Average 8-12 words, some inversion for emphasis
- **Level 3:** Average 12-18 words, complex with multiple clauses
- **Level 4:** Average 18-25+ words, literary word order
- **Level 5:** Variable complex structures, dense paragraphs

### Content Density
- **Level 1:** One clear concept per article, repetition of key terms
- **Level 2:** 2-3 related concepts, moderate detail
- **Level 3:** 3-5 concepts, cultural depth, analytical thinking
- **Level 4:** Complex historical/cultural analysis, nuance
- **Level 5:** Scholarly analysis, cultural interpretation, debate

## GUIDELINES FOR BOTH OPTIONS

**Accuracy:**
- All Portuguese text must be authentic and from verified sources
- Translations must be natural English, not literal word-for-word
- Difficulty assessments must be based on objective linguistic criteria
- Include source citations when available

**Completeness (Option A):**
- Include 20-30 articles across all 5 difficulty levels
- Ensure balanced representation of content types
- Provide clear reasoning for each difficulty assignment
- Include articles from diverse sources (blogs, news, academic, cultural organizations)

**Completeness (Option B):**
- Include full article text or substantial excerpt (minimum 300 words)
- **Extract and explain EVERY SINGLE WORD** — no word should be left unexplained
  - This includes: articles (o, a), prepositions (em, de, pra), conjunctions (e, ou), contractions (na, do, pra)
  - Even "common" or "obvious" words must have explanations
  - For Level 1-2 articles, this might mean 50-100+ vocabulary entries
  - For Level 3+ articles, this might mean 100-200+ vocabulary entries
- Provide 3-5 grammar patterns with examples
- Include 5+ comprehension questions at varying cognitive levels
- Provide 5+ practice exercises

**Difficulty Assessment Standards:**
- Use the 5-level framework consistently across all articles
- Base assessment on: vocabulary frequency, sentence complexity, grammar forms, content density, background knowledge required
- Provide numerical data (word count, unique words, sentence length averages)
- Justify each level with specific linguistic features

**Clarity:**
- Explain difficulty levels in terms a beginner can understand
- For Option A: Make it easy to scan and select appropriate reading material
- For Option B: Break down complex grammar and cultural references
- Assume the reader knows ZERO Portuguese

**Critical: Example Sentences & Practice**
- **Only use words that have already been taught in the vocabulary glossary**
- Do NOT introduce new vocabulary in example sentences, grammar explanations, or practice exercises
- If an example requires a word not yet taught, EITHER:
  - Use a different example with only taught vocabulary, OR
  - Teach the new word on-the-fly with full explanation (word, meaning, pronunciation, part of speech, gender if noun)
- Example of BAD practice: "The island is beautiful" (introduces "beautiful" without teaching)
- Example of GOOD practice: "The fish jumped" (uses only taught vocabulary)
- Avoid compound structures (like "X is Y") unless those verbs were explicitly taught in the article vocabulary
- All grammar explanations must use examples with 100% known vocabulary

**Tone:**
- Respectful of Capoeira culture and history
- Educational, encouraging engagement with authentic materials
- Celebrate the richness of Capoeira cultural knowledge
- Make articles feel accessible, not intimidating
